The BuzzFire F-A-Q

Do I need to register to use BuzzFire?

No, all you need is a Twitter account. Then click Sign In and you'll be logged in via Twitter and ready to use BuzzFire!

Does that mean you'll be able to know my Twitter account password?!

No, we won't. What happens when you sign in from BuzzFire is you tell Twitter that you want to allow us to access your Twitter data. Twitter gives us a token to do that, not your password. At anytime, you're free to revoke that token from your Twitter settings.

What's this site for?

BuzzFire is a simple app that allows you to bookmark your favorite tweets, and 'like' the tweet. Tweets that get a lot of bookmarks and likes make it to a simple scoreboard on BuzzFire. On top of that, you can post comments on the tweets.

Why should I use BuzzFire?

Well, feeds from Twitter can be overwhelming, especially if you're a hot rock star wannabe who follows 1,324,3242 people on Twitter! We help you keep tabs of the tweets that matter to you on your journey to stardom!

How come the question I want to ask isn't listed here?

Maybe it's too obscure. You're welcome to email us and we'll be happy to answer you.

Who are you guys? Why did this come about?

BuzzFire is a result of Django Dash 2010, a mad 48 hour sprint to make an app in Django. The two guys who teamed up for this are Choon Kee Oh and Kenny Shen respectively. We're just 2 web development loving guys from Singapore.
